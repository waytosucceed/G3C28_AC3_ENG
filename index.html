A word's meaning is what a word tells us or represents. It's like a secret code that helps us understand what someone is talking about. When we know what a word means, we can use it to share our thoughts and ideas.
How do you create a sentence from that word?
To create a sentence from a word:

  1. Think about what the word means.
  2. Imagine a situation where you might use this word.
  3. Put the word into a group of other words that tell a tiny story or share an idea.
  4. Make sure your sentence makes sense.

The author's Purpose means the reason why the author wrote a story or a piece of text.
Here are three simple reasons why an author might write something:

  • To Entertain: This means the author wants to make the reader have fun or enjoy the story.
    For example, a funny story or an adventure tale is written to entertain.
  • To Inform: This means the author wants to teach the reader something new or give information.
    For example, a book about animals or a how-to guide is written to inform.
  • To Persuade: This means the author wants to convince the reader to think or do something.
    For example, an ad trying to sell a toy or a letter asking people to recycle is written to persuade.
